USGS Bird Banding Analysis Visualization

Explanation of Project

Welcome to our Analysis of The USGS Bird Banding project, where we embarked on an journey to analyze this extensive and ecologically important data gathering project. Our analysis utilizes the vast amounts of program information from the USGS Bird Banding Program. We have created visualizations for four unique species of birds (The American Flamingo, the Ruby-Throated Hummingbird, the Northern Saw-Whet Owl, and the Blue Winged Teal) including a banding location map, a month vs. location heatmap, and a chart to show locational data. These visualizations offer dynamic perspectives on bird movement, illuminating the nuances of their behavior and adaptation. Additionally, the user-driven interaction features of our visualizations page empower enthusiasts and researchers alike to customize their exploration, enabling them to select specific bird species for detailed analysis.
